But how does my router pick up signal?

Your WiFi signal is transmitted using radio waves. There are two main steps: The device's wireless adapter translates data into a radio signal. The wireless router receives the signal, decodes it, and sends information to the Internet using its physical Ethernet connection.

But wait how do we get WiFi from space?

Satellite ISPs like Viasat work in essentially the same three-step fashion, but instead of a central station, the internet signal is delivered via fiber to a large antenna (called an “earth station”) on the ground, which then moves the signal via radio waves up to our satellite.
